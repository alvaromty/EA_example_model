Appears In: Stereotyping
Stereotypes are an extension mechanism of the Unified Modeling Language, that can be used to create a new element type based on an existing UML element type.  <br/><br/>There are many cases where defining a new type to model a thing of interest in a particular industry or domain, can be very beneficial.  Stereotyped elements often add clarity to a model, by making the elements more meaningful and recognizable to the audience.  The use of images and shapes can also make the resulting models more readily understood by the audience.  <br/>Examples of stereotypes include a 'contract' stereotype in the legal profession, a 'meter' stereotype in the energy distribution industry or a 'stakeholder requirement' in the business analysis profession.<br/><br/>Care should be taken not to overuse the facility as it can lead to fragmentation of meaning and can also result in models that are not easily understood by external audiences.<br/><br/>
