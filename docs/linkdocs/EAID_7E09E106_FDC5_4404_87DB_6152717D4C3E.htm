Executive Summary

This business case outlines how the extended selling platform will address the existing issues with the selling model and uses the opportunities that exist in the technology stack. It analyzes a number of options to improve the declining sales issue but recommends the extended selling platform as the best and most viable option.

Issue

While the move some twelve years ago to online selling of titles in the form of books and articles increased the revenue and gave the organisation a leading position in the market sales have been steadily declining over the last three quarters. This trend does not look like improving and an expensive marketing drive has very small effect.

Figure 1: Declining Sales over 2016.

Anticipated Outcomes

Extended selling will change the way that potential customers view the organisation changing it from an online book store to a major retail force and this will attract a wider range of customers to the site and leverage of the substantial investment in logistic infrastructure and technology.

It is envisioned that profits will increase by at last 37% in the first year of operation and  a further 11% in subsequent years.

Recommendations

A variety of options were analyzed and the extended selling was chosen because of its simple implementation path and its ability to leverage a large number of existing services and infrastructure. Many technology mechanisms had been built with this in mind so little needs to be done to change the way they work.

The marketing is relatively straight forward and the purchase of a number of variety stores has already paved the way for the move.
